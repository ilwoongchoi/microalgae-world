 There are two different categories of microalgae:-
1. Eukaryotic Microalgae
2. Cyanobacteria

Eukaryotic Microalgae are literally eukaryotic single-celled microalgae, while cyanobacteria are prokaryotic microalgae that can also be found in chains of single-cells. There are some types of heterotrophic eukaryotic microalgae, but all cyanobacteria are autotrophic. Heterotrophic and autotrophic are opposite concepts. Heterotrophic means to acquire energy through uptaking nutrients from extra-cellular sources, while autotrophic means they can produce their own energy sources through photosynthesis. On this page I will provide some examples of eukaryotic microalgae and cyanobacteria.
1. Eukaryotes


Chlorella vulgaris


Chlorella vulgaris is a well known species of microalgae that is often manufactured into nutritional supplements for intakes by people. It is a photosynthetic freshwater species and contains high amount of chlorophyll, which gives it its green colour. It also has high protein content, often ranging upto 50~60% of its weight.


Nannochloropsis salina


Nannochloropsis salina is a marine microalgal species that is photosynthetic. Like Chlorella it is high in chlorophyll content and is also well know for its EPA content. EPA stands for eicosapentanoic acid, which is a type of polyunsaturated fatty acid found in many living organisms and is very beneficial for humans.


Porphyridium cruentum


Porphyridium cruentum is a marine microalgae. It is a photosynthetic microalgae and produces exopolysaccharides that is widely utilized in cosmetic industry as thickening and gelling agents. It also produces phycoerythrin, a type of red-pigment that can be used as fluorescent marker in biomedical research.


Schizochytrium limacinum


Schizochytrium limacinum is a marine heterotrophic microalga that has a capability to produce significant amount of polyunsaturated acid, especially DHA. DHA stands for docosahexanoic acid, and is a type of omega-3 acid, along with EPA. It is highly beneficial for human health, being a building block of human brain, eye and skin.



2. Cyanobacteria


Spirulina(Arthrospira platensis)


Arthrospira platensis, also widely known as Spirulina, is a marine cyanobacteria well known to the public as health supplements. It is extremely high in protein and phycocyanin, the latter being a pigment which gives it the blue colour. Phycocyanin is also an antioxidant with very strong capacity to scavenge free radicals.


Microcystis aeruginosa


Microcystis aeruginosa is a species of cyanobacteria that is the main contributor to algal blooms that becomes rampant during the summer months. It produces a toxin called microcystin, which is a harmful substance that can cause a lot of wildlife to die and is also harmful to human skin.
